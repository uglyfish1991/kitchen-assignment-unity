Introduction

"Using the prefabs you created in the course, build a kitchen layout of your own design"

We were tasked with creating a kitchen layout, similar to the guided session. While I have done some programming, visuals, scene building, and navigating 3D elements were new to me. To take away the design element so I could focus on the practical skills, I chose to try and recreate my own home kitchen as closely as I could. I tried to recreate my kitchen to scale, using floorplan measurements, but there were some limitations to what I could achieve based on the prefabs / models I had access to, and my current knowledge of Unity. The images shown have walls missing you you can see from different angels, so some backs of models are exposed.

Hierarchy

The hierarchy was helpful for keeping my game objects organised. Some things - like the "window wall" were made up of many cubes. Keeping them all in a parent object made it easier to apply changes to the whole wall in one go!

Evaluation

While I know this isn't good, I'm very pleased with it. As my first effort in working in a 3D space, and creating a recognisable scene, I think I've done a good job. Most people who have been in my kitchen recognised the 3D version! Colours and general placement of prefabs matched my kitchen well - and while there are inaccuracies, they're not terribly jarring!

I think I used the materials and colours well. We set up a lot of them in the lesson, though I modified some (like the granite) to match my kitchen a bit more. I also brought in some others from a resource Keith shared, but I'm not sure I used them. I found working with the materials and textures of some prefabs a bit confusing, but I think I got there in the end!

There are many improvements to be made - but I won't be overly critical with the knowledge this is my first try. There are some gaps and alignment issues that I couldn't quite iron out. I also think the scale isn't quite right, though it's not too far off. (My kitchen is very small). I think there are some inconsistencies with the models used. For example, the surfaces and cupboard look quite real, while the kettle is obviously a lower poly model, and this is quite clashy.

Further Work

With more time, I would like to:

  • Add more decorations to the scene to make it look more lived in
  • Source or create a proper window. The current window is made of many cubes, and I don't think this is a good approach
  • Fix the tiles so they reflect the actual kitchen. They stop too soon, and are too rectangular.

Lessons / Takeaways

My biggest take away from the project is how difficult I found navigating the 3D space to be. Placing and aligning objects seems very difficult, even with snapping. A surface could look quite well placed from on angle, and the a rotation of your camera shows you're actually half a mile away! I need to work in 3D spaces more to understand the axes more.
